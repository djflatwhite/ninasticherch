01

Toasted gloves, 2024, photography, 3D animation
What use do found objects in a holiday home have in swiss nature?

02

Eluminatoria, 2024, 3D modeling, animation, type design
The Deluminator, was a magical device used by Albus Dumbledore to remove light sources from the immediate vicinity of the Deluminator, but also to bestow them.
I extracted shapes from deep-sea creatures that live in complete darkness. A combination of these shapes created the characters for my product.

03

Plants, 2024, 3D animation, AI generated images, video, in collaboration with Joëlle Järmann
In a one week workshop with Massimiliano Audretsch and Jamie Waterfall we delved deeper into the topic of plants. With this project, we wanted to expand our skills with AI image generation.

04

Echofera VI, 2023, editorial design, photography, video, creative coding, in collaboration with Nanda Gysi
Echofera VI is a planet consisting only of sounds and vibration, which was discovered by scientists in 2064. Due to the planet's frequency fluctuations, the weather changes, which could be captured visually with the help of Chladni figures. We created the story about our planet with the help of AI.

05

Kapi, 2023, type design, edits by Binta Kopp, pictures by davitschi
"Kapi" is serbocroatian and translates to "drop". The artist Milena Patagônia incorporates her secons language for the first time in her EP.
Due to this, the logo and the type of the album cover was created with hot glue.

06

Kulturesk art festival, 2024, visual identity, in collaboration with Miro Jakob
We won the open call to design the campaign for the first "Kulturesk" festival of the arts, which took place over three days in Bern, CH. The idea of a pulsating movement emerged as a key visual to convey "we are here". The 3D plan on the programme folding poster was really important to us.

07

Ich sammle, 2024, editorial design
*this book contains sensitive content, including, for example, relics of the nazi era*
A book about the controversial art collector Bruno Stefanini. During his lifetime he wanted to bring back order to his collection, but he didn't manage to do this,
his collecting habits became increasingly chaotic. This resulted in the layout of the editorial design.
